🔒Login and PIN setup
1

Go to the Startup Portal

Visit the portal URL and click "Startup Login". Enter your Startup ID (format: CTIE-YYYY-NNN, given to you by CTIE admin) and choose a PIN.

2

First login sets your PIN permanently

On your very first login, whatever PIN you enter becomes your permanent portal PIN. Use at least 4 digits. Choose something you will remember -- there is no "forgot PIN" link. Contact ctie [at] kletech.ac.in to reset if needed.

3

Your session stays alive for 8 hours

Once logged in, you stay logged in for 8 hours even if you navigate between pages or refresh. Closing the browser tab logs you out. Do not share your PIN with anyone outside your core team.

📄Posting an internship role
Posting takes under 4 minutes. Fill in the structured form -- the better your JD, the more relevant the applications you receive. Your role goes live immediately.

Discipline and qualification fields

Select which engineering or management disciplines are relevant. This becomes a filter that students use on the browse page. Selecting "Any" means all students can see and apply for your role.

Must-have vs preferred skills

Must-have skills are shown with a filled badge -- students understand they need all of these. Preferred skills have an outline badge -- nice to have, not mandatory. Being clear here reduces mismatched applications.

CGPA cutoff

Set a minimum CGPA if required. This is shown as a badge on your internship card so students self-filter before applying. Leave at 0 for no cutoff.

Note: students still apply even below your cutoff -- you decide in review.

Stipend vs Academic Internship

Select Stipend-Based and enter the monthly amount in Rupees, or select Academic Internship for curriculum-credit / learning-based roles with no monetary compensation. Both appear on the portal -- students filter by their preference.

👤Managing applicants
1

Review applications

Go to your portal > Applicants tab. See all applications in a table with resume links inline. Click Shortlist (sends candidate a notification) or Reject (sends a polite decline email). You have 7 working days to review.

2

Schedule the interview

After shortlisting, click Schedule for any candidate. Set date, time, mode, and paste a Google Meet link if online. The student gets a calendar invite on both their email addresses automatically.

3

Enter outcome within 48 hours

After the interview date passes, click Outcome for that candidate. Choose Selected, Not Selected, or Reschedule (if applicable). You must do this within 48 hours of the interview.

Selected triggers an OTP to the student's institute email for acceptance.

4

Wait for OTP confirmation

Once you mark Selected, the student gets a 6-digit OTP. When they confirm it, their status changes to Selected and you can begin onboarding. You are notified by email when they confirm.

↻Reschedule rules -- read carefully
Maximum 2 reschedules per application. No exceptions. This rule exists to protect students' time. The system enforces it at three points -- you cannot override it once the limit is reached.

Reschedule 1 -- allowed, no warning

You can reschedule an interview once without any consequences. The student is notified with the new slot details and a note showing "Reschedule 1 of 2 used".

Reschedule 2 -- allowed, with final warning

A second reschedule is permitted, but both you and the student see a prominent warning: "This is the final reschedule. No further rescheduling permitted." If this interview does not proceed, the application will be closed.

Reschedule 3 -- blocked automatically The system blocks the third reschedule attempt. The application is auto-closed, the student is notified with a clear explanation, CTIE admin is alerted, and your Reschedule Strike count increments. CTIE management can see this.

Reschedule from outcome screen

If you conducted the interview but need to see the student again, you can also select "Reschedule" as the outcome on the outcome entry screen. This still counts toward the 2-reschedule limit.

📊Logging weekly progress
Every Monday, you receive an automatic reminder to log the previous week's progress for each active intern. This feeds into CTIE's impact reporting and the student's completion certificate.

What to log each week

Days present (out of 5 or 6), tasks assigned, tasks completed, a performance rating (1-5 stars), and optional comments for the intern. The student can see this feedback in their tracker.

Marking internship complete

When the internship ends, go to Active Interns > select the intern > Mark Complete. Set the end date, give a final rating and short feedback. The system generates a completion certificate and emails it to both the student's addresses immediately.

❓Frequently asked questions
I forgot my PIN. How do I reset it? ▼
Email ctie [at] kletech.ac.in from your registered startup contact email. CTIE admin will reset your PIN hash in the system and you can set a new PIN on next login.
Can I edit an internship after posting? ▼
Currently you cannot edit a live posting directly. If you need to make a significant change, close the existing role and post a corrected one. Minor corrections can be done by contacting ctie [at] kletech.ac.in.
A student confirmed but is now not joining. What do I do? ▼
Contact ctie [at] kletech.ac.in. The admin can reset the application status so you can select another candidate. The confirmed position count will also be adjusted.
How many roles can I post at the same time? ▼
There is no hard limit. However, please post only roles you genuinely intend to fill. Unused postings that expire without any reviews reflect poorly on your startup's reliability score.
Can students from outside Karnataka apply? ▼
Yes. The portal is open to students from any college across India. If you prefer local students, set the Institute Preference to "Local to Hubballi / Dharwad" when posting -- this is shown on your card so distant students know upfront.
