🎯How the process works
1

Browse and apply

No account needed to browse. Find a role that interests you and click Apply. Fill in your details, upload your resume (PDF, max 5MB), and write 2-3 sentences on why you are interested. Submit.

You get an Application ID immediately. Save it.

2

Startup reviews your application

The startup sees your resume and application inline. They will shortlist or pass within 7 working days. If shortlisted, you get an email on both your addresses.

If no action within 14 days, your application closes automatically. You will be notified and are free to apply elsewhere.

3

Interview gets scheduled

The startup sets a date, time, and mode (online/in-person). You receive a calendar invite to both your emails with all details including the Google Meet link if online.

Join 2 minutes early. Keep your resume ready.

4

Outcome within 48 hours

After your interview, the startup must enter their decision within 48 hours. You will see the status change in your tracker. If selected, you proceed to the OTP confirmation step.

5

Confirm acceptance with OTP

If selected, a 6-digit OTP is sent to your institute email only. Enter it on the confirmation page within 10 minutes to confirm your acceptance. This is your binding confirmation -- no paperwork needed.

6

Complete your internship

Work with the startup. They log your weekly progress. When done, they mark it complete and you receive a verified Internship Completion Certificate on both your email addresses.

🔒Why we need your institute email
What is an institute email? Your institute email is the one provided by your college or university. It typically ends in .edu.in, .ac.in, or the institution's own domain. Examples: yourname@kletech.ac.in, rollno@vtu.edu.in
Why we require it (not Gmail or Yahoo) Your institute email proves you are currently enrolled. Anyone can create a Gmail account. Only a current student has access to a valid institute email. This protects startups from fake applications and protects you -- it ensures fair competition.
What your personal email is used for We also collect your personal email (Gmail etc.) separately. This is used only for post-graduation contact -- when your institute email expires after you graduate, startups can still reach you. Your completion certificate also goes to both addresses.
The OTP always goes to institute email only When a startup selects you, the 6-digit confirmation OTP is sent exclusively to your institute email. This ensures only you -- not someone with just your personal email -- can confirm the offer. Your personal email gets a notification to check the institute inbox.
🔐The OTP confirmation -- what it is and why
What is an OTP? OTP stands for One-Time Password. It is a 6-digit code sent to your institute email when a startup selects you. You enter it on a confirmation page. This is how you formally accept the internship offer -- no physical signature, no PDF form.
1

Check your institute email inbox (and spam)

The OTP email comes from ctie [at] kletech.ac.in with subject line containing "Action required: Confirm your internship offer". Check spam if you don't see it.

2

Click the confirmation link in the email

The email contains a large "Confirm Acceptance" button that takes you to the OTP entry page. You can also go to the Track page and follow the prompt.

3

Enter the 6 digits within 10 minutes

The OTP is valid for only 10 minutes. If it expires, use the Resend button on the verification page -- a new OTP is sent. You have 5 attempts before the code locks. If locked, the startup needs to re-select you.

Security warning KLE-CTIE will NEVER call you, WhatsApp you, or message you asking for your OTP. Do not share the OTP with anyone -- not even the startup. The OTP is for your eyes only.

🏆Your rights as an applicant
The system protects your time
Day 7 after applying
Startup is automatically reminded to review your application.
Day 14 with no action
Application closes, you are notified, and you can apply elsewhere.
Day 5 after shortlist, no interview
Startup is reminded to schedule your interview.
48 hours after interview, no outcome
Startup gets a second urgent nudge. Your status shows "Decision overdue".
96 hours after interview, no outcome
Application closes, CTIE admin is alerted, you can apply elsewhere.
Reschedule limit A startup can reschedule your interview a maximum of 2 times. On the second reschedule, both you and the startup are warned that no further rescheduling is allowed. A third attempt is blocked and the application closes automatically.
❓Frequently asked questions
Can students from any college apply? ▼
Yes. The portal is open to students from any college across India. You do not need to be from KLE Technological University. The only requirement is a valid institute-provided email address.
What if my institute email has expired or I have graduated? ▼
If your institute email has expired, you cannot apply to new roles. However, your personal email on file will still receive completion certificates and any communications from startups you have completed internships with. Contact ctie [at] kletech.ac.in if you have a special situation.
I lost my Application ID. What do I do? ▼
Check your confirmation email -- it was sent to both your institute and personal email when you applied. Search for "Application received" or "KLE-CTIE". If you still cannot find it, email ctie [at] kletech.ac.in with your name and the role you applied for.
Can I apply for multiple internships at the same time? ▼
Yes. You can apply to multiple open roles simultaneously. Each application has its own ID and tracks independently. You cannot apply to the same role twice.
The startup is not responding. What should I do? ▼
The system automatically nudges startups and auto-closes applications when SLA deadlines are missed. You do not need to do anything. If you feel something is genuinely wrong beyond the SLA window, email ctie [at] kletech.ac.in with your Application ID.
Is there a fee to use this portal? ▼
No. The KLE-CTIE Internship Portal is completely free for students. No registration fee, no placement fee, nothing. If anyone claims otherwise, report it immediately to ctie [at] kletech.ac.in.
Is the completion certificate official / recognised? ▼
The completion certificate is issued by KLE-CTIE, a DST-NIDHI recognised Technology Business Incubator under KLE Technological University. It carries a unique certificate ID and can be verified through CTIE. It is accepted as a valid internship credential by most universities for credit.
